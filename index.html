Test Overview

What to expect on exam day

Structure

The test has two sections: 33 algebra and functions questions (46 minutes) followed by 36 reading comprehension questions (51 minutes). You cannot return to a section once time expires.

No Calculator

All math must be done by hand. Questions test your understanding of equations, polynomials, functions, graphing, and number series — not your ability to punch buttons.

Scoring

Results are reported as a stanine score from 1 to 9. A minimum score of 4 is typically required to advance to the interview stage, though local JATCs set their own cutoff.

How to Use This Site

A simple 5-step plan to get exam-ready

  1. Diagnose your starting point. Take the full practice test to see which topics need the most work.
  2. Strengthen weak areas. Work through the algebra lessons with SVG diagrams, then hit the 570-question bank for targeted drill.
  3. Practice under timed conditions. Use a stopwatch and do not use a calculator. Simulate test-day pressure.
  4. Review every mistake. Read the explanations carefully. Understanding why an answer is wrong is more valuable than getting one right by luck.
  5. Retake the full test. Score yourself again and confirm you are consistently above the minimum threshold before your real exam date.

Community Insights

Tips from actual applicants on r/IBEW and the Ultimate Electrician's Guide

  • Wait times vary by local. Some locals process applications in weeks, others take months. Apply early and to multiple locals if possible.
  • Interview carries weight. The aptitude test is a gatekeeper, but your interview score often determines your rank on the list.
  • Algebra is the differentiator. Most test-takers pass reading. The math section separates those who advance from those who retake.
  • No calculator means mental math. Practice arithmetic without a calculator — especially fractions, percentages, and order of operations.
