1. Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

When an expression has more than one operation, you must follow a specific order to get the correct answer. The acronym PEMDAS helps you remember:

P — Parentheses first
E — Exponents (powers, square roots)
MD — Multiplication & Division (left to right)
AS — Addition & Subtraction (left to right)

Key rule: Multiplication and division have equal priority — work them left to right. Same for addition and subtraction.

Example: Evaluate 8 + 2 × 5

Multiplication comes before addition:
8 + 2 × 5 = 8 + 10 = 18

Common mistake: adding first gives 10 × 5 = 50, which is wrong.

Example: Evaluate 20 ÷ 4 × 3

Division and multiplication have equal priority — go left to right:
20 ÷ 4 × 3 = 5 × 3 = 15

Common mistake: multiplying first gives 20 ÷ 12 = 1.67, which is wrong.

Practice Questions

Q1 — PEMDAS
Evaluate: 3 + 6 × (5 + 4) ÷ 3 − 7
Answer: B) 14

Step-by-step:
  1. Parentheses: 5 + 4 = 9 → 3 + 6 × 9 ÷ 3 − 7
  2. Multiplication & Division (left to right): 6 × 9 = 54 → 3 + 54 ÷ 3 − 7
  3. 54 ÷ 3 = 18 → 3 + 18 − 7
  4. Addition & Subtraction (left to right): 3 + 18 = 21 → 21 − 7 = 14
Q2 — PEMDAS
Evaluate: (12 − 4)² ÷ 8 + 3 × 2
Answer: A) 14

Step-by-step:
  1. Parentheses: 12 − 4 = 8 → 8² ÷ 8 + 3 × 2
  2. Exponent: 8² = 64 → 64 ÷ 8 + 3 × 2
  3. Multiplication & Division (left to right): 64 ÷ 8 = 8; 3 × 2 = 6 → 8 + 6
  4. Addition: 8 + 6 = 14
Q3 — PEMDAS
Evaluate: 48 ÷ 2(3 + 5) − 6
Answer: A) −3

Step-by-step:
  1. Parentheses first: 3 + 5 = 8 → 48 ÷ 2(8) − 6
  2. Implicit multiplication (juxtaposition) like 2(8) has higher priority than the ÷ sign on standardized tests. Evaluate 2(8) as a unit: 2 × 8 = 16 → 48 ÷ 16 − 6
  3. Division: 48 ÷ 16 = 3 → 3 − 6
  4. Subtraction: 3 − 6 = −3

Note: On the IBEW test, expressions like a(b) are treated as a single grouped term, so 2(8) is evaluated before the division.

2. Evaluating Algebraic Expressions

Evaluating an expression means substituting given numbers for variables and then simplifying using PEMDAS. This is one of the most common question types on the IBEW test.

Example: Evaluate 3x² − 2xy + 5y when x = 2 and y = −1

Substitute: 3(2)² − 2(2)(−1) + 5(−1)

= 3(4) − 2(2)(−1) + 5(−1)
= 12 − (−4) + (−5)
= 12 + 4 − 5
= 11

Example: The formula for the area of a trapezoid is A = ½(b₁ + b₂)h. Find A when b₁ = 8, b₂ = 12, h = 5.

A = ½(8 + 12) × 5
= ½(20) × 5
= 10 × 5
= 50

Key tip: Always use parentheses when substituting negative numbers. For example, if x = −3, write 2(−3), not 2 − 3 — they give very different results!

Practice Questions

Q1 — Evaluating Expressions
Evaluate 2a² − 3b + 4c when a = 3, b = 5, c = −2
Answer: A) −5

Step-by-step:
  1. Substitute: 2(3)² − 3(5) + 4(−2)
  2. Exponent: 2(9) − 3(5) + 4(−2)
  3. Multiply: 18 − 15 − 8
  4. Subtract left to right: 18 − 15 = 3; 3 − 8 = −5
Q2 — Formula Evaluation
The formula C = 5/9(F − 32) converts Fahrenheit to Celsius. Find C when F = 68.
Answer: C) 20°

Step-by-step:
  1. Substitute: C = 5/9(68 − 32)
  2. Parentheses: 68 − 32 = 36 → C = 5/9 × 36
  3. Multiply: 5 × 36 = 180; 180 ÷ 9 = 20
Q3 — Multi-Variable Substitution
Evaluate (x² − y²) ÷ (x + y) when x = 7 and y = 4
Answer: B) 3

Step-by-step:
  1. Substitute: (7² − 4²) ÷ (7 + 4)
  2. Exponents: (49 − 16) ÷ (11)
  3. Parentheses: 33 ÷ 11 = 3

Note: (x² − y²) = (x + y)(x − y), so this simplifies to x − y = 7 − 4 = 3.

Q4 — Official ETA Sample
A = B + 3(4 − C). If B = 5 and C = 2, what is the value of A?
Answer: B) 11

Step-by-step:
  1. Substitute B = 5 and C = 2: A = 5 + 3(4 − 2)
  2. Parentheses first: 4 − 2 = 2 → A = 5 + 3(2)
  3. Multiply: 3 × 2 = 6 → A = 5 + 6
  4. Add: A = 11

3. Solving Linear Equations

A linear equation in one variable can be written in the form ax + b = c. The goal is to isolate the variable using inverse operations.

Golden Rule: Whatever you do to one side of the equation, you MUST do to the other side.

Steps for Solving

  1. Simplify each side (distribute, combine like terms).
  2. Move variable terms to one side using addition/subtraction.
  3. Move constant terms to the other side.
  4. Divide by the coefficient of the variable.
  5. Check your answer by plugging it back in.

Example: Solve 5x − 7 = 3x + 9

Step 1: Subtract 3x from both sides → 2x − 7 = 9
Step 2: Add 7 to both sides → 2x = 16
Step 3: Divide both sides by 2 → x = 8
Check: 5(8) − 7 = 40 − 7 = 33 ✓ and 3(8) + 9 = 24 + 9 = 33 ✓

Practice Questions

Q1 — Two-Step Equation
Solve for x: 4x + 11 = 35
Answer: B) x = 6

Step-by-step:
  1. Subtract 11 from both sides: 4x = 35 − 11 → 4x = 24
  2. Divide both sides by 4: x = 24 ÷ 4 = 6
Check: 4(6) + 11 = 24 + 11 = 35 ✓
Q2 — Variables on Both Sides
Solve for y: 7y − 3 = 2y + 17
Answer: C) y = 4

Step-by-step:
  1. Subtract 2y from both sides: 5y − 3 = 17
  2. Add 3 to both sides: 5y = 20
  3. Divide by 5: y = 4
Check: 7(4) − 3 = 28 − 3 = 25 ✓ and 2(4) + 17 = 8 + 17 = 25 ✓
Q3 — Multi-Step with Distribution
Solve for x: 3(x − 4) + 2x = 18
Answer: B) x = 6

Step-by-step:
  1. Distribute: 3x − 12 + 2x = 18
  2. Combine like terms: 5x − 12 = 18
  3. Add 12 to both sides: 5x = 30
  4. Divide by 5: x = 6
Check: 3(6 − 4) + 2(6) = 3(2) + 12 = 6 + 12 = 18 ✓

4. Systems of Equations

A system of equations is a set of two or more equations with the same variables. The IBEW test focuses on systems of two linear equations with two unknowns. There are two main methods:

Method 1: Substitution

Solve one equation for one variable, then substitute that expression into the other equation.

Example: Solve by substitution

y = 2x − 1
3x + 2y = 12

Substitute y into the second equation:
3x + 2(2x − 1) = 12
3x + 4x − 2 = 12
7x = 14 → x = 2
Then y = 2(2) − 1 = 3
Solution: (2, 3)

Method 2: Elimination (Addition)

Add or subtract equations to eliminate one variable, then solve for the remaining variable.

Example: Solve by elimination

2x + 3y = 11
2x − y = 7

Subtract the second equation from the first:
(2x + 3y) − (2x − y) = 11 − 7
4y = 4 → y = 1
Substitute into either equation: 2x + 3(1) = 11 → 2x = 8 → x = 4
Solution: (4, 1)

Practice Questions

Q1 — Systems (Substitution)
Solve the system:
y = x + 5
2x + y = 11
Answer: B) (2, 7)

Step-by-step (Substitution):
  1. Substitute y = x + 5 into 2x + y = 11: 2x + (x + 5) = 11
  2. Combine: 3x + 5 = 11
  3. Subtract 5: 3x = 6 → x = 2
  4. Then y = 2 + 5 = 7
  5. Solution: (2, 7)
Q2 — Systems (Elimination)
Solve the system:
3x + 2y = 19
x − 2y = −7
Answer: C) (3, 5)

Step-by-step (Elimination):
  1. Add the equations to eliminate y: (3x + 2y) + (x − 2y) = 19 + (−7)
  2. 4x = 12 → x = 3
  3. Substitute into x − 2y = −7: 3 − 2y = −7
  4. −2y = −10 → y = 5
  5. Solution: (3, 5)
Q3 — Systems (Word Problem)
Two numbers add to 25. Their difference is 7. Find both numbers.
Answer: B) 16 and 9

Step-by-step:
  1. Let the numbers be x and y. Then x + y = 25 and x − y = 7.
  2. Add the equations: (x + y) + (x − y) = 25 + 7
  3. 2x = 32 → x = 16
  4. Then y = 25 − 16 = 9
  5. The numbers are 16 and 9. Check: 16 + 9 = 25 ✓, 16 − 9 = 7 ✓

5. Simplifying Polynomials

A polynomial is an expression with one or more terms involving variables and exponents. Simplifying means combining like terms and applying the distributive property.

Combining Like Terms

Like terms have exactly the same variable raised to exactly the same power. Combine them by adding or subtracting their coefficients.

Example: Simplify 5x² + 3x − 7 + 2x² − 4x + 10

Group like terms:
x² terms: 5x² + 2x² = 7x²
x terms: 3x − 4x = −x
Constants: −7 + 10 = 3
Result: 7x² − x + 3

Distributive Property

The distributive property: a(b + c) = ab + ac. Multiply the outside term by each term inside the parentheses.

Example: Simplify 4(2x − 3) − 2(x + 5)

Distribute: 8x − 12 − 2x − 10
Combine like terms: 8x − 2x = 6x; −12 − 10 = −22
Result: 6x − 22

Practice Questions

Q1 — Combining Like Terms
Simplify: 6a + 4b − 3a + 7b − 5
Answer: A) 3a + 11b − 5

Step-by-step:
  1. Group a terms: 6a − 3a = 3a
  2. Group b terms: 4b + 7b = 11b
  3. Constant stays: −5
  4. Result: 3a + 11b − 5
Q2 — Distributive Property
Simplify: 5(3m − 2) + 4(2m + 1)
Answer: A) 23m − 6

Step-by-step:
  1. Distribute: 5(3m) + 5(−2) + 4(2m) + 4(1) = 15m − 10 + 8m + 4
  2. Combine like terms: 15m + 8m = 23m
  3. Combine constants: −10 + 4 = −6
  4. Result: 23m − 6
Q3 — Distributive with Subtraction
Simplify: 2(x² + 3x) − 3(x² − x + 2)
Answer: A) −x² + 9x − 6

Step-by-step:
  1. Distribute: 2x² + 6x − 3x² + 3x − 6
    (Careful: −3(−x) = +3x and −3(2) = −6)
  2. Combine x² terms: 2x² − 3x² = −x²
  3. Combine x terms: 6x + 3x = 9x
  4. Constant: −6
  5. Result: −x² + 9x − 6

6. Factoring Polynomials

Factoring is the reverse of multiplying — you break a polynomial into a product of simpler expressions. The IBEW test covers three main types:

Type 1: Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

Find the largest factor that divides every term, then factor it out.

Example: Factor 12x³ − 18x² + 6x

GCF of coefficients 12, 18, 6 is 6.
GCF of variables: the smallest power of x is x.
GCF = 6x. Factor it out:
6x(2x² − 3x + 1)

Type 2: Difference of Squares

a² − b² = (a + b)(a − b)

Example: Factor x² − 49

Recognize: x² is a perfect square, 49 = 7².
x² − 49 = x² − 7² = (x + 7)(x − 7)

Type 3: Factoring Quadratics (x² + Bx + C)

Find two numbers that multiply to C and add to B.

Example: Factor x² + 7x + 12

Need two numbers that multiply to 12 and add to 7.
Pairs of factors of 12: (1,12), (2,6), (3,4) — 3 + 4 = 7 ✓
x² + 7x + 12 = (x + 3)(x + 4)

Example: Factor x² − 5x − 24

Need two numbers that multiply to −24 and add to −5.
Factors of 24: (1,24), (2,12), (3,8), (4,6).
For product −24, one must be negative. Try −8 and +3: −8 × 3 = −24 ✓, −8 + 3 = −5 ✓
x² − 5x − 24 = (x − 8)(x + 3)

Practice Questions

Q1 — Greatest Common Factor
Factor completely: 15x⁴ − 10x³ + 25x²
Answer: A) 5x²(3x² − 2x + 5)

Step-by-step:
  1. GCF of coefficients (15, 10, 25): 5
  2. GCF of variables (x⁴, x³, x²): x² (smallest exponent)
  3. GCF = 5x²
  4. Divide each term: 15x⁴ ÷ 5x² = 3x²; −10x³ ÷ 5x² = −2x; 25x² ÷ 5x² = 5
  5. Result: 5x²(3x² − 2x + 5)
Q2 — Difference of Squares
Factor: 4x² − 81
Answer: A) (2x − 9)(2x + 9)

Step-by-step:
  1. Recognize: 4x² = (2x)² and 81 = 9²
  2. Apply difference of squares: a² − b² = (a − b)(a + b)
  3. 4x² − 81 = (2x)² − 9² = (2x − 9)(2x + 9)
Q3 — Factoring a Quadratic
Factor: x² + 2x − 35
Answer: D) (x + 7)(x − 5)

Step-by-step:
  1. Need two numbers that multiply to −35 and add to +2.
  2. Try factors of 35: (5, 7). For product −35, one is negative.
  3. +7 and −5: multiply = −35 ✓, sum = 7 + (−5) = 2 ✓
  4. Result: (x + 7)(x − 5)

7. FOIL Method — Multiplying Binomials

FOIL stands for First, Outer, Inner, Last — it's your method for multiplying two binomials like (a + b)(c + d).

(a + b)(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd
First × First | Outer × Outer | Inner × Inner | Last × Last

Example: Multiply (x + 3)(x + 5)

F: x × x = x²
O: x × 5 = 5x
I: 3 × x = 3x
L: 3 × 5 = 15
Combine: x² + 5x + 3x + 15 = x² + 8x + 15

Example: Multiply (2x − 3)(x + 4)

F: 2x × x = 2x²
O: 2x × 4 = 8x
I: −3 × x = −3x
L: −3 × 4 = −12
Combine: 2x² + 8x − 3x − 12 = 2x² + 5x − 12

Practice Questions

Q1 — FOIL Method
Multiply: (x + 6)(x − 2)
Answer: A) x² + 4x − 12

Step-by-step:
  1. F: x × x = x²
  2. O: x × (−2) = −2x
  3. I: 6 × x = 6x
  4. L: 6 × (−2) = −12
  5. Combine: x² − 2x + 6x − 12 = x² + 4x − 12
Q2 — FOIL Method
Multiply: (3x − 4)(2x + 5)
Answer: A) 6x² + 7x − 20

Step-by-step:
  1. F: 3x × 2x = 6x²
  2. O: 3x × 5 = 15x
  3. I: −4 × 2x = −8x
  4. L: −4 × 5 = −20
  5. Combine: 6x² + 15x − 8x − 20 = 6x² + 7x − 20
Q3 — Official ETA Sample (FOIL + Distributive)
y = 3(x + 5)(x − 2). Which formula is equivalent to this?
Answer: A) y = 3x² + 9x − 30

Step-by-step:
  1. First FOIL the two binomials: (x + 5)(x − 2)
  2. F: x × x = x² | O: x × (−2) = −2x | I: 5 × x = 5x | L: 5 × (−2) = −10
  3. Combine: x² − 2x + 5x − 10 = x² + 3x − 10
  4. Now multiply everything by 3: 3(x² + 3x − 10) = 3x² + 9x − 30

8. Functions & Graphs

A function is a rule that assigns each input (x) exactly one output (y). The most common form on the IBEW test is the linear function:

y = mx + b
m = slope (rate of change, rise over run)
b = y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis)

Finding Slope

m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)

Example: Find the slope of the line through (2, 5) and (6, 13)

m = (13 − 5) / (6 − 2) = 8 / 4 = 2
The line rises 2 units for every 1 unit it runs to the right.

Using a Table of Values

You can find the function rule from a table by identifying the pattern.

Example: Find the equation from this table

x0123
y471013

Each time x increases by 1, y increases by 3 → slope m = 3.
When x = 0, y = 4 → y-intercept b = 4.
Equation: y = 3x + 4

Practice Questions

Q1 — Slope Calculation
Find the slope of the line passing through points (1, 2) and (5, 14).
Answer: B) 3

Step-by-step:
  1. m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)
  2. m = (14 − 2) / (5 − 1) = 12 / 4 = 3
Q2 — Finding Equation from Table
Find the equation for this table:
x0123
y−22610
Answer: A) y = 4x − 2

Step-by-step:
  1. Find slope: when x goes from 0→1, y goes from −2→2, change = +4 → m = 4
  2. Check: 1→2, y goes 2→6, change = +4 ✓
  3. y-intercept: when x = 0, y = −2 → b = −2
  4. Equation: y = 4x − 2
Q3 — Interpreting Slope
A plumber charges a $50 service fee plus $25 per hour. Which equation gives the total cost C for h hours?
Answer: B) C = 25h + 50

Step-by-step:
  1. Fixed fee (y-intercept, b) = $50
  2. Rate per hour (slope, m) = $25 per hour
  3. C = mh + b = 25h + 50
Q4 — Official ETA Sample (Function Analysis)
a = ½b − 4. Which statement is true?
Answer: A) When b < 8, a is negative

Step-by-step:
  1. Find when a = 0: ½b − 4 = 0 → ½b = 4 → b = 8
  2. When b = 8, a = 0. When b < 8, a is negative. When b > 8, a is positive.
  3. Test with b = 6: a = ½(6) − 4 = 3 − 4 = −1 (negative ✓)
  4. Test with b = 10: a = ½(10) − 4 = 5 − 4 = +1 (positive)
  5. Therefore: When b < 8, a is negative is the correct statement.
Q5 — Official ETA Sample (Equation from Table)
Which equation represents the same relationship as this table?
x0123456
y−5−4−3−2−101
Answer: B) y = x − 5

Step-by-step:
  1. When x = 0, y = −5 → y-intercept b = −5
  2. When x increases by 1 (from 0→1), y goes from −5→−4, change = +1 → slope m = 1
  3. Check: x = 2, y = −3 → y = 1(2) − 5 = −3 ✓
  4. Check: x = 6, y = 1 → y = 1(6) − 5 = 1 ✓
  5. Equation: y = x − 5

9. Number Series

Number series questions test your ability to recognize patterns. The IBEW test often includes sequences where you must find the next term. Here are the main pattern types:

Pattern Type 1: Constant Difference (Arithmetic)

Each term is obtained by adding (or subtracting) the same number.

Example: 3, 7, 11, 15, 19, ___

Difference: +4, +4, +4, +4 → Next term: 19 + 4 = 23

Pattern Type 2: Constant Ratio (Geometric)

Each term is obtained by multiplying (or dividing) by the same number.

Example: 2, 6, 18, 54, ___

Ratio: ×3, ×3, ×3 → Next term: 54 × 3 = 162

Pattern Type 3: Alternating Patterns

Two different rules alternate between terms.

Example: 1, 10, 2, 9, 3, 8, ___

Odd positions: 1, 2, 3 (adding 1 each time)
Even positions: 10, 9, 8 (subtracting 1 each time)
Next term is at position 7 (odd): 3 + 1 = 4

Pattern Type 4: Second-Level Differences

The differences between terms form their own pattern.

Example: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, ___

These are squares: 1², 2², 3², 4², 5² → Next: 6² = 36
(Also see: differences are 3, 5, 7, 9 — increasing by 2 each time → next difference is 11 → 25 + 11 = 36)

Practice Questions

Q1 — Arithmetic Series
What comes next? 12, 17, 22, 27, 32, ___
Answer: B) 37

Step-by-step:
  1. Find the difference: 17 − 12 = 5, 22 − 17 = 5, etc.
  2. Constant difference of +5
  3. Next term: 32 + 5 = 37
Q2 — Geometric Series
What comes next? 5, 15, 45, 135, ___
Answer: C) 405

Step-by-step:
  1. Find the ratio: 15 ÷ 5 = 3, 45 ÷ 15 = 3, 135 ÷ 45 = 3
  2. Constant ratio of ×3
  3. Next term: 135 × 3 = 405
Q3 — Alternating Pattern
What comes next? 2, 20, 4, 18, 6, 16, ___
Answer: B) 8

Step-by-step:
  1. Split into two interleaved sequences:
  2. Odd positions: 2, 4, 6 — pattern: +2 each step → next odd (position 7) = 8
  3. Even positions: 20, 18, 16 — pattern: −2 each step
  4. The next term is the 7th term (odd position): 8
Q4 — Second-Level Differences
What comes next? 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, ___
Answer: C) 21

Step-by-step:
  1. First differences: 3−1=2, 6−3=3, 10−6=4, 15−10=5
  2. The differences are: 2, 3, 4, 5 — increasing by 1 each time
  3. Next difference: 6
  4. Next term: 15 + 6 = 21

These are triangular numbers: Tₙ = n(n+1)/2. T₆ = 6×7/2 = 21.

Q5 — Official ETA Sample (Alternating Pattern)
What comes next? 110, 112, 107, 109, 104, ___
Answer: C) 106

Step-by-step:
  1. Look at the pattern of changes from one term to the next:
  2. 112 − 110 = +2 | 107 − 112 = −5 | 109 − 107 = +2 | 104 − 109 = −5
  3. The pattern alternates: +2, −5, +2, −5, +2, −5...
  4. Next operation: 104 + 2 = 106

10. Exponents & Rational Numbers

Rules of Exponents

Product Rule: xᵃ × xᵇ = xᵃ⁺ᵇ
Quotient Rule: xᵃ ÷ xᵇ = xᵃ⁻ᵇ
Power Rule: (xᵃ)ᵇ = xᵃˣᵇ
Zero Exponent: x⁰ = 1 (x ≠ 0)
Negative Exponent: x⁻ᵃ = 1/xᵃ
Product to Power: (xy)ᵃ = xᵃyᵃ

Example: Simplify (2x³)(5x⁴)

Multiply coefficients: 2 × 5 = 10
Add exponents: x³ × x⁴ = x³⁺⁴ = x⁷
Result: 10x⁷

Example: Simplify x⁸ / x³

Subtract exponents: x⁸⁻³ = x⁵

Fractions, Decimals & Ratios

Fraction Operations Reminder

Addition/Subtraction: Find a common denominator first.
Multiplication: Multiply numerators, multiply denominators.
Division: Multiply by the reciprocal (flip the second fraction).
Simplifying: Divide numerator and denominator by their GCF.

Example: Simplify 3/4 + 2/3

Common denominator of 4 and 3 is 12.
3/4 = 9/12, 2/3 = 8/12
9/12 + 8/12 = 17/12 = 1 5/12

Practice Questions

Q1 — Exponent Rules
Simplify: (3x²y)(4x³y⁵)
Answer: B) 12x⁵y⁶

Step-by-step:
  1. Multiply coefficients: 3 × 4 = 12
  2. Add exponents for x: x² × x³ = x²⁺³ = x⁵
  3. Add exponents for y: y¹ × y⁵ = y¹⁺⁵ = y⁶
  4. Result: 12x⁵y⁶
Q2 — Rational Numbers
A recipe calls for 2/3 cup of sugar. You want to make half the recipe. How much sugar do you need?
Answer: B) 1/3 cup

Step-by-step:
  1. Half of 2/3 = 2/3 × 1/2
  2. Multiply numerators: 2 × 1 = 2
  3. Multiply denominators: 3 × 2 = 6
  4. Result: 2/6 = 1/3 cup (simplified)

11. The Cartesian Plane

The Cartesian plane (coordinate plane) is a grid defined by the x-axis (horizontal) and y-axis (vertical). Every point is identified by an ordered pair (x, y).

Key facts:
  • The origin is (0, 0) — where the axes cross.
  • The x-coordinate tells you how far left or right to go.
  • The y-coordinate tells you how far up or down to go.
  • Quadrant I: (+, +) — Quadrant II: (−, +) — Quadrant III: (−, −) — Quadrant IV: (+, −)

Plotting Points

Example: Plot the points A(3, 4), B(−2, 5), C(−3, −1), D(1, −2)

A(3, 4): Start at origin, go right 3, up 4. (Quadrant I)
B(−2, 5): Go left 2, up 5. (Quadrant II)
C(−3, −1): Go left 3, down 1. (Quadrant III)
D(1, −2): Go right 1, down 2. (Quadrant IV)

Graphing a Line

To graph y = 2x − 1:

  1. Plot the y-intercept (b = −1): point (0, −1).
  2. Use the slope (m = 2 = 2/1): from (0, −1), go up 2, right 1 to (1, 1).
  3. Connect the points with a straight line.

Example: Find the equation of a horizontal line through (4, 3)

A horizontal line has slope m = 0. All points have the same y-coordinate.
Equation: y = 3

Example: Find the equation of a vertical line through (−2, 5)

A vertical line has an undefined slope. All points have the same x-coordinate.
Equation: x = −2

Practice Questions

Q1 — Plotting Points
In which quadrant does the point (−5, 7) lie?
Answer: B) Quadrant II

Step-by-step:
  1. x = −5 (negative), y = 7 (positive)
  2. Negative x, positive y → Quadrant II

Quadrant II is the upper-left region of the plane.

Q2 — Graphing a Line
What is the y-intercept of the line y = −3x + 8?
Answer: C) 8

Step-by-step:
  1. The equation is in slope-intercept form: y = mx + b
  2. Here m = −3 and b = 8
  3. The y-intercept is b = 8, meaning the line crosses the y-axis at (0, 8)
